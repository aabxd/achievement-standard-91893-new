Qualify for the Future.

Welcome

NCEA Guide is a website dedicated to making your secondary Highschool experience a smooth one. This website covers the essentials of the National Certificate of Educational Achievement or 'NCEA' for short which is the pathway to New Zealand's universities.

The NZ curriculum

The New Zealand Curriculum sets the direction for teaching and learning in English-medium New Zealand schools. Students will partake in NCEA Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 during Years 11, 12, and 13.

Process

NCEA is a process that primarily revolves around secondary schools to prepare students for University. Students partake in assessments where they will be marked on the quality of their work. These assessments are composed of 'Internals' which are marked by the school, and 'Externals' which are marked by the NZQA organization. By completing these assessments, Students will gain credits. These credits are mandatory in passing levels. There are 3 NCEA Levels (Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3) in secondary schools that students must pass, especially Level 3, in order to gain Univeristy Entrance. There are four possible grades a student can get in partaking an assessment: Not Achieved, Achieved, Achieved with Merit, and Achieved with Excellence. Credits gained above the 'Not Achieved' grade will contribute to passing NCEA levels. A set amount of credits is needed to pass a certain level. In Level 2 and Level 3, credits from the previous level can be used to pass that level.
