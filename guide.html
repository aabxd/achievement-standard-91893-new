Where do I start?

This guide covers all you need to know to ensure a smooth transition from Junior Years to NCEA and NCEA to University Entrance

Read More

Junior Years

The first two years of New Zealand Highschool are about developing the essential skills and work quality for NCEA. There is an opportunity to take part in NCEA during junior years by applying for a senior subject or partake in Internals and/or Externals. This can be done through the teacher's discretion. Subjects such as Mathematics might have certain pre-requisites in order to gain entry to the senior subject.

The National Certificate of Educational Achievement

Once students complete their junior years in highschool, They are eligible to be involved in NCEA. In NCEA, students will gain credits through assessments known as 'Internals' and 'Externals'. Externals generally come in the form of an exam. The remaining three years of secondary highschool will be dedicated to passing NCEA Levels 1, 2 and 3. The list below shows the requirements in passing each level:

  • Level 1: 80 credits
  • Level 2: 80 credits (60 Level 2 credits, 20 from Level 1)
  • Level 3: 80 credits (60 Level 3 credits, 20 from Level 2)

Students doing NCEA are able to work across multiple levels regardless of grade, Though they are highly encouraged to spend a year passing each level.

Endorsements

Endorsements of a subject can be attained by reaching a certain amount of credits. Subjects can be endorsed with Merit (composed of both Merit and Excellence credits) and Excellence. At least 3 credits from an Internal and an External must be attained in order to get a Subject Endorsement.

Reconsiderations

If the student isn't compliant with their final grade in an external, they can be reassessed in a 'reconsideration'. Reconsiderations cost $30 NZD which is refunded if the student receives a higher grade. As of now, some assessments may have an opportunity to do a 'resubmission' for a higher grade should the quality of the student's work be borderline with the higher grade.

University Entrance

The prerequisites for most University Entrances include:

  1. Achieved NCEA Level 3
  2. 10 Literacy Credits (5 reading & 5 writing) which can be attained from L2 and L3
  3. 14 credits from three University Entrance approved subjects
  4. 10 Numeracy credits from Level 1 and above

Should the student not meet these requirements, the NCEA credits gained from level 3 will be used to tally up a 'Guaranteed Entry Score' for entry into a undergraduate programme. The score will be calculated by awarding points as follows:

  • Excellence = 4 points
  • Merit = 3 points
  • Achieved = 2 points

The minimum Gauranteed Entry Score for all undergraduate degrees is 150 points. A total of 180 points is required for the Bachelor of Architectural Studies and the Bachelor of Building Science. Only a maximum of 24 credits from a UE approved subject can be counted for GES.
